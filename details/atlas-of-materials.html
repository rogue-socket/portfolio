Research Interface

Atlas of Materials

A searchable reference browser for a materials studio working across samples, provenance, supplier notes, and editorial annotation without falling back to a spreadsheet feel.

Role
Product design, taxonomy planning, prototype front-end, and interface writing.
Scope
Search patterns, metadata hierarchy, sample browsing, component language, and review workflows.
Stack
HTML, CSS, vanilla JavaScript, structured content models, and low-friction documentation.
Outcome
The team moved from scattered references to a shared index that supported critique, sourcing, and handoff.

Challenge

Make technical material readable

The system needed to present chemistry, sourcing, and visual nuance without the atmosphere of a procurement tool.

Structure

Browse by family and provenance

Entries were arranged around material families, tags, suppliers, and notes so browsing could happen from several directions.

Interaction

Use filters without visual noise

Filtering, related links, and metadata stayed lightweight so the main reading flow remained calm and legible.

Result

Support reviews with less translation

Design and sourcing teams could discuss materials in one place instead of passing screenshots, PDFs, and side notes around.
