Archive Prototype

Field Notes Archive

A browsing system for interviews, transcripts, workshop notes, and tags designed to feel like an annotated reading room rather than a file dump.

Role
Research synthesis, archive structure, interaction design, and front-end prototyping.
Scope
Source metadata, quote extraction, browse states, relationship mapping, and editorial summary blocks.
Stack
Static front-end patterns, structured note fields, search planning, and lightweight validation passes.
Outcome
Researchers could move between raw observations and higher-level synthesis without losing source context.

Challenge

Avoid turning notes into debris

Interview material was scattered across folders and documents, which made retrieval and comparison slow.

Structure

Link source, summary, and theme

The archive balanced direct source access with curated summaries so detail and synthesis stayed connected.

Interaction

Support scanning and close reading

Rows, tags, and concise summaries made quick scanning possible, while deeper records remained only one step away.

Result

Reuse fieldwork with more confidence

The archive became a shared reference for later strategy, service design, and writing instead of a dead repository.
